My Top 5 Board Games

Quicklinks

Dominion Zombicide Betrayal 7 Wonders Gloomhaven

1. Dominion

  • Pros:
    1. High replayability thanks to randomized game features and countless expansions.
    2. Simple core rules make it easy for new players to learn.
    3. Good for casual play and competitive play alike, with good cards for both.
  • Cons:
    1. Player interaction can feel low depending on the number of Attack cards in play.
    2. Requires frequent shuffling and card management.
    3. Some card mixes can lead to the game feeling stale if not varied enough.

2. Zombicide

  • Pros:
    1. Stong Zombie hunting theme with cooperative gameplay that makes you feel like you're the hero.
    2. Survival thrill that keeps players on the edge of their seats.
    3. Loads of plastic minis, weapons, and scenarios, giving a big table presence and plenty of ways to mow down hordes.
  • Cons:
    1. Rules can be clunky at times, with a steeper learning curve and some unclear or fiddly sections.
    2. Gameplay can feel repetitive, long, and quite random, especially with constant dice rolling and similar-feeling missions.
    3. Balance issues: difficulty can swing between too easy and brutally punishing, with scaling and XP rules sometimes making one player overpowered while others lag behind.

3. Betrayal at House on the Hill

  • Pros:
    1. Great for social groups who enjoy narrative moments and funny/creepy situations more than tight strategy, often feeling like a horror movie night.
    2. Very high replayability from randomized tiles, omens, and dozens of different haunts, so stories feel different each time.
    3. Strong horror theme and atmosphere, with exploring a haunted mansion, creepy events, and a midgame “haunt” twist that flips the story into a showdown.
  • Cons:
    1. Has a learning curve for new players due to the complexity of the rules and scenarios.
    2. Extremely random and swingy: tile layout, item draws, and which haunt triggers can make the game accidentally too easy, impossibly hard, or abruptly short.
    3. Traitor/hero balance varies wildly, which can be frustrating if the players are more competitive.

4. 7 Wonders

  • Pros:
    1. Simultaneous play supports 3-7 players with zero downtime and consistent 30-minute games.
    2. Multiple victory paths (science, military, economy, wonders) create strategic variety and replayability.
    3. Fun drafting mechanics that create tough choices between keeping cards and denying opponents
  • Cons:
    1. Setup can be very tedious, especially with expansions requiring card sorting by player count.
    2. Iconography and science scoring mechanics have a learning curve for new players.
    3. The theme can feel very hit or miss depending on the player's interests.

5. Gloomhaven

  • Pros:
    1. Incredible replayability from 95+ scenarios, 17 unique classes, and branching story that evolves over 100+ hours.
    2. Innovative card-based combat with positioning, elements, and combos rewards smart planning and team synergy.
    3. Persistent progression through leveling, retiring characters, and unlocks creates a living RPG campaign.
  • Cons:
    1. Massive component count leads to nightmare setup, teardown, and organization without third-party aids.
    2. Long playtime can be a barrier for casual gamers or those with limited time.
    3. Complex rules and mechanics may overwhelm new players and require a dedicated group to fully enjoy.
